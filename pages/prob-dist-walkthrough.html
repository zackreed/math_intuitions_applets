üéØ Introduction

A probability distribution describes how values of a random variable are distributed. For continuous random variables, we use a probability density function (PDF), where the area under the curve over an interval gives the probability that the random variable falls in that interval.

Key Properties of PDFs:

  • Non-negative: \(f(x) \geq 0\) for all \(x\)
  • Normalized: \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)\,dx = 1\)
  • Mean (Expected Value): \(\mu = E[X] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x f(x)\,dx\)
  • Variance: \(\sigma^2 = E[(X - \mu)^2] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (x - \mu)^2 f(x)\,dx\)

This explorer includes six important distributions:

  • Normal (Gaussian): The famous bell curve, used extensively in statistics
  • Exponential: Models waiting times and lifetimes
  • Uniform: All values in a range are equally likely
  • Gamma: Generalizes the exponential distribution
  • Cauchy: Has undefined mean and variance, heavy-tailed
  • Laplace: Double exponential, sharper peak than normal

üìö Mathematical Formulas

Normal Distribution

\[f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^2}\]

Parameters: \(\mu\) (mean), \(\sigma\) (standard deviation)

Mean: \(\mu\), Variance: \(\sigma^2\)

Exponential Distribution

\[f(x) = \begin{cases} \lambda e^{-\lambda x} & x \geq 0 \\ 0 & x < 0 \end{cases}\]

Parameter: \(\lambda\) (rate parameter)

Mean: \(\frac{1}{\lambda}\), Variance: \(\frac{1}{\lambda^2}\)

Uniform Distribution

\[f(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{1}{b-a} & a \leq x \leq b \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}\]

Parameters: \(a\) (minimum), \(b\) (maximum)

Mean: \(\frac{a+b}{2}\), Variance: \(\frac{(b-a)^2}{12}\)

Gamma Distribution

\[f(x) = \frac{x^{\alpha-1} e^{-x/\beta}}{\beta^\alpha \Gamma(\alpha)} \quad \text{for } x > 0\]

Parameters: \(\alpha\) (shape), \(\beta\) (scale)

Mean: \(\alpha\beta\), Variance: \(\alpha\beta^2\)

Cauchy Distribution

\[f(x) = \frac{1}{\pi\gamma\left[1 + \left(\frac{x-x_0}{\gamma}\right)^2\right]}\]

Parameters: \(x_0\) (location), \(\gamma\) (scale)

Mean: undefined, Variance: undefined

Laplace Distribution

\[f(x) = \frac{1}{2b} e^{-\frac{|x-\mu|}{b}}\]

Parameters: \(\mu\) (location), \(b\) (scale)

Mean: \(\mu\), Variance: \(2b^2\)

üåç Real-World Applications

Normal Distribution

  • Heights and weights: Human physical characteristics often follow normal distributions
  • Test scores: Standardized test results are often normalized
  • Measurement errors: Random errors in scientific measurements
  • Financial returns: Daily stock returns (approximately)

Exponential Distribution

  • Radioactive decay: Time until an atom decays
  • Service times: Time to serve a customer in a queue
  • Component lifetimes: Time until a machine part fails
  • Time between events: Arrivals at a service counter

Uniform Distribution

  • Random number generation: Foundation for simulations
  • Round-off errors: In numerical computations
  • Uncertainty modeling: When all values are equally likely
  • Sampling: Random selection from a finite interval

Gamma Distribution

  • Wait times: Time until k events occur in a Poisson process
  • Insurance claims: Aggregate claim amounts
  • Rainfall modeling: Monthly precipitation amounts
  • Queue theory: Total service time for multiple customers
