üéØ Introduction

A geometric series is a sum where each term is a fixed fraction of the previous term. In this visualization, we repeatedly subdivide a rectangle, filling half of the remaining area at each step. This demonstrates how an infinite series can converge to a finite value.

Key Concept: Monotonic Bounded Sequences

A sequence is monotonic increasing if each term is greater than or equal to the previous term. If the sequence is also bounded above (never exceeds some maximum value), then it must converge to a limit.

\(\text{If } a_1 \leq a_2 \leq a_3 \leq \cdots \leq M\text{, then } \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n \text{ exists}\)

In our geometric series, the partial sums keep growing but never exceed the total area \(ab\) of the rectangle!

üîó Connection to Calculus

This geometric series visualization demonstrates the Monotone Convergence Theorem:

Monotone Convergence Theorem

If a sequence \(\{a_n\}\) is monotonic (always increasing or always decreasing) and bounded, then it converges to a limit.

In our example:

  • Monotonic: \(S_1 \leq S_2 \leq S_3 \leq \cdots\) (each partial sum is larger than the previous)
  • Bounded: \(S_N \leq ab\) for all \(N\) (partial sums never exceed total area)
  • Converges: \(\lim_{N \to \infty} S_N = ab\)

The general formula for a geometric series with first term \(a\) and ratio \(r\) (where \(|r| < 1\)) is:

\(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n = \frac{a}{1-r}\)

In our rectangle, we're computing \(\sum_{n=1}^{N} \frac{ab}{2^n} = ab\sum_{n=1}^{N} \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n\), which approaches \(ab\) as \(N \to \infty\).
